Installed Requirements
Requirement 1:
- Install all the font files found in the "ForzaFont" folder (preferred)
Requirement 2:
- Download & Install Simhub
- Go through the initial first launch process and set-up SimHub as you wish (units, colors, games etc)
- Configure the UDP and Telemetry settings for Forza Motorsport 2023
Requirement 3:
- In Forza's Gameplay & Hud Settings disable/turn OFF the following:
- Lap Time, Position, Score, Pit Stops
- Speedometer
- Car Progression
- Drift HUD
- Quick Action
- Track Mastery
- Race Objectives
- Rewind Notification
Optional ones:
- Track Caution Messages
- Race Activity Feed
- Race Regulation Messages
Now onto the Overlays
Manual Method (longer, for individual overlays)
Step 1:
- Double Click on one of the Overlays in "Overlay-Modules". Deselect the "Install Dash Fonts"
- Click "Import into SimHub library"
Step 2:
- Great now we have succesfully imported our first Overlay
- Now in SimHub head over into the "Dash Studio"
- Then click on "Overlays"
Step 2a:
- Open our trusty "Module-alignment-tool.png" found in "Overlay-Modules" folder
- Fullscreen/Set as Desktop background image this picture (Background is easier)
- If you are opening it in Fullscreen you'll need to change tabs with Alt+Tab.
- Lastly on SimHub, click on "New Overlay Layout"
Step 3:
- Now you are succesfully into the Overlay Layout Editor.
- Click on "Add Overlay"
- Then click on the "+" sign of the Overlay you wish to add. And it'll be displayed with a Translucent background to distinguish it.
- With your mouse, drag it so that the top left corner of the element is in line with the top left corner of the allocated space in the Aligment tool.
- Last step, hover over the right edge of the Translucent box and the mouse will change to a resize icon. Simply reduce the size of the overlay until it is neatly contained within the allocated space
- Keep repeating step 3 until you're satisfied/have added all the Overlays you want
- When you're done adding the desired overlays, click "OK"
- Now "Save Layout" and give it the name that you wish to use
Step 4:
- If your Default Overlay Renderer is HTML, we'll need to change the Renderer for the Tachometer.
- Load your Overlay
- Click "Edit Layout"
- Now you'll need to change the Renderer set for the Tachometer.
- Click on the Gear Icon for the Tachometer.
- Set the Renderer for it to "WPF Render Engine".
